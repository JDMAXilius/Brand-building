## Audience
- **Primary Audience**: Two core buyers, both premium — buying certainty and capability, not the cheapest bid.
  1. **Technical founders / CEOs** (seed–Series B) at AI-forward, deep-tech companies — need to build or scale a credible product fast without losing control or equity.
  2. **Enterprise CTO / CIO / Chief Innovation Officers** — need to move AI from pilot to production, pass security and compliance review, and show measurable outcomes under board scrutiny.
- **Buyer Titles**: Founder/CEO, CTO/VP Eng, CIO, Chief Innovation Officer.
- **Key Problem**: Clients need elite AI and software engineering they can't build in-house — to a standard that holds from a founder's first release to an enterprise security review — without hiring an agency that overpromises and hands off juniors.

## Positioning
- **Differentiation**: A premium engineering partner that ships its own software products — so it builds clients' systems like an owner, not a vendor. It refuses the two easy businesses (the cheap outsourcer that ships and vanishes; the deck-and-invoice consultancy that never touches production) and commits to the rarer one: senior people who build and run real products, applied to clients' problems with full ownership.
- **Positioning Statement**: Brand A is the premium AI & software engineering partner that ships its own products — so it builds yours like an owner, to a standard that holds from a founder's first release to an enterprise security review.
- **Map Axes**: **X** — execution vendor ←→ strategic partner that owns outcomes. **Y** — consulting-only ←→ also ships own products. Brand A sits **top-right**: high ownership *and* actually ships products — a quadrant consultancies (no products) and dev shops (no ownership) can't occupy.
- **Competitors**: Thoughtworks & premium consultancies (craft, consulting-only — no owned products); Impekable / ELEKS (product-dev agencies); LeewayHertz & AI MVP studios (fast, commoditizing on speed); Accenture / Slalom (scale generalists, variable seniority). See `../COMPETITIVE_LANDSCAPE.md` §4–5.
- **Market Position**: Premium. Senior-only, selective, owned outcomes — depth over headcount.

## Values & Mission
- **Core Values**:
  1. **Engineering Excellence** — production-grade or it doesn't ship.
  2. **Ownership** — embed, own outcomes, tell the truth (including when an idea should change).
  3. **Applied AI** — AI where it earns its place in production, never as a buzzword.
  4. **Security by Default** — real security standards from the first commit.
  5. **Craft** — quality in the parts no one sees as much as the parts they do.
- **Vision**: To be the engineering partner ambitious companies call when software is too important to get wrong — built on senior craft, applied AI, and a refusal to ship anything less than production-grade.
- **Mission**: Engineer the AI-powered software clients can't build alone — from a founder's first product to an enterprise's hardest integration — and stand behind every system in production.
