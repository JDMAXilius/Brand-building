## 01 — VOICE OVERVIEW

Brand B sounds like a senior creative-technologist who has actually shipped worlds — someone equally at home describing the goosebumps and the frame budget. It is vivid about the experience and exact about the engineering, and it has no patience for empty hype because it has watched too many dazzling demos die under real load. It speaks to creative and technical buyers at once: paint the scene, then prove it with a number. The reel and the running build carry the confidence; the words just point at them.

**Voice Essence:** *Brand B sounds like someone who has shipped worlds — vivid about the experience, exact about the engineering, allergic to hype.*

## 03 — VOICE QUALITIES

**Vivid** — Paints a scene, then gets out of the way.
- **Do:** "A crowd of 200 moving through one persistent world, 60 fps, no seams."
- **Don't:** "An amazing, immersive, next-level experience."

**Exact under the magic** — Names the engine, the frame budget, the player count, the latency.
- **Do:** "Built in Unreal; 60 fps locked; 200 concurrent guests."
- **Don't:** "Cutting-edge tech powering it all."

**Confident, not loud** — Lets the reel and the build carry it.
- **Do:** "Live on the floor since spring."
- **Don't:** "The most revolutionary attraction ever made!"

**Player/guest-first** — Centers what the audience feels and does, not the toolchain.
- **Do:** "What the guest remembers is the moment the world reacted to them."
- **Don't:** A paragraph about your pipeline before the experience.

**Honest about the hard parts** — Netcode, scale, and live-ops are where projects die; say so.
- **Do:** "Concurrency is the risk. Here's how we hold it."
- **Don't:** Pretend multiplayer is easy.

## 04 — VOCABULARY

**Words we use:** world · real-time · immersive · multiplayer · netcode · live-service · frame budget · fidelity · persistent · scale · ship · build · play · engine (Unreal / Unity) · performance · on the floor

**Words we avoid:** "next-level," "AAA-quality" (as an empty boast), "revolutionary," "metaverse" (as filler), "seamless," "magical" (overused), and any claim a reel can't back.

**Brand-specific language:** Talk about **worlds**, **experiences**, and **systems** — and insist they're the same thing well-made. Distinguish a **reel/demo** from a **shipped build** (live, under load). Center the **guest** (attractions) or **player** (games). Name the **engine** and the **numbers**.

---

## 05 — WRITING STYLE RULES

- **Lead with the experience, follow with the build.** Scene first, then the metric.
- **Specifics over adjectives** — numbers, engines, player counts, latency.
- **Sentence length:** Short, punchy lines welcome; vary for momentum. Em-dashes fine.
- **Punctuation:** Exclamation marks rare and earned — let the work be loud, not the copy.
- **Voice:** Active. Show, don't tell — pair claims with footage or metrics.
- **Capitalization:** Sentence case; reserve emphasis for the work.

## 07 — BEFORE AND AFTER

**Before:** "Our revolutionary metaverse platform delivers next-level immersive experiences."
**After:** "A persistent world for 200 concurrent guests at 60 fps — built in Unreal, live on the floor."
*Why:* Trades empty superlatives for a scene plus the numbers that prove it.

**Before:** "We make amazing, seamless multiplayer games."
**After:** "Server-authoritative multiplayer we own end to end — 64 players, sub-80 ms, anti-cheat in."
*Why:* Names the hard systems instead of gesturing at quality.

**Before:** "An unforgettable, magical guest experience."
**After:** "The moment the world reacts to the guest — and keeps reacting, every day, on the floor."
*Why:* Specific emotional payoff + the reliability promise.

**Before:** "Cutting-edge tech powers our AAA-quality worlds."
**After:** "Unreal, real-time GI, a 16 ms frame budget held under load."
*Why:* Replaces boast-words with the engineering that earns them.

---

## 08 — THINGS WE NEVER SAY

- **Empty superlatives:** "next-level," "AAA-quality" (as a boast), "revolutionary," "magical."
- **Filler buzzwords:** "metaverse" as a catch-all, "seamless," "immersive" with nothing behind it.
- **Claims a reel or a build can't back** — performance, concurrency, or fidelity we can't show.
- **Toolchain-first openings** that bury the experience.
- **Exclamation-mark hype** — the work is loud enough.
- **Pretending the hard parts are easy** — netcode, scale, and live-ops get named, not hidden.
