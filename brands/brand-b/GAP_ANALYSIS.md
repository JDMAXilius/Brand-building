## 3 — WHAT TO ADD (prioritized, on-strategy)

1. **Art production** — the biggest missing revenue line and effectively table-stakes in this space
   (Side, Devoted, Theory, Pingle all lead with it). Frame it premium: high-end characters,
   environments, animation, cinematics, technical art — *art that ships into a running system*, not
   a render farm. Without it, Brand B can't credibly do "full real-time-3D experiences."
2. **QA / playtesting — premium & automated.** Don't compete on Side's volume QA; differentiate
   with **engineering-led, automation/AI-assisted** QA and playtesting (the Side+Razer direction)
   — fits the "built like systems" story (test it like a system).
3. **Porting + performance optimization (productized).** Enludio sells these as clean entry
   services; they're a natural, lower-commitment on-ramp that shows off Brand B's engineering and
   leads to bigger builds. Make both explicit, named services.
4. **Spatial computing / computer vision — explicit.** Brand B implies XR but should name MR/VR/CV
   the way Enludio does, to win the immersive/attraction buyer head-to-head.
5. **Advisory / consultation.** A premium "we'll architect this with you" engagement — high-margin,
   relationship-opening, and aligned with "partner, not body shop."
6. **Audio — add or partner.** Lower priority; partner first (specialist studios), bring in-house
   only if it becomes core.

## 4 — PARTNER, DON'T BUILD
- **Hardware / motion platforms** (Talon's lane). Brand B is a software/engineering company;
  partner for hardware (the Enludio↔Talon model) rather than diluting into manufacturing.

## 5 — DELIBERATELY SKIP (and why)
These are Side's commodity, volume plays. Adding them would pull Brand B into the full-service
outsourcer lane its positioning explicitly rejects (`BRAND_STRATEGY.md` §10):
- **Localization / Loc QA · Player support · Community management · Datasets/AI-data · Massive
  multi-studio scale.** Each is a scale/headcount business, not a craft+engineering differentiator.
  Skipping them is a *positioning choice*, not a capability gap — say so internally.

## 6 — WEBSITE / PRESENCE GAPS (what their sites have that ours will need)
*(Aligned with the "show the work, not social proof" posture — no client logos/testimonials.)*
- **Portfolio / showreel of our own work** — the single most important page; the reel *is* the
  pitch. (Every reference leads with work.)
- **Clear services pages** — enumerate capabilities (above) so buyers self-identify. Competitors'
  sites are explicit menus; Brand B's offer is currently only in strategy docs.
- **Careers / team page** — this is a talent-driven industry; an established senior bench is a
  selling point and a recruiting engine. Signals "real company," not newcomer.
- **Engine / platform credibility** — Unreal/Unity partner status or engine badges *when true*.
- **Behind-the-build / technical content** — optional; "show the work" thought content (how we held
  64-player netcode under load) builds authority the way Companion/Side technical posts do.
- **Not** client logos, testimonials, reviews, or "trusted by" walls — per brand posture.
