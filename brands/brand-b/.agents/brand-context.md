## Audience
- **Primary Audience:** Theme-park / attraction Experience & Creative Directors and location-based-entertainment (LBE) operators; game-studio CTOs / Technical Directors and publishers. Buyers who need spectacle that also runs.
- **Buyer roles:** Experience / Creative Director (attractions, LBE); Head of Operations (attraction); Game-studio CTO / Technical Director; Executive Producer; Publisher technical lead.
- **Key Problem:** Immersive and multiplayer projects routinely dazzle in a demo and then break under real load on opening day. Buyers are forced to stitch together a creative studio (spectacle, no systems rigor), a co-dev shop (capacity, no partnership), and a backend/multiplayer specialist (systems, no experience) — and own the integration risk themselves.
- **Their Language:** "It has to feel real and run flawlessly every single day on the floor." · "I need senior engineers who can own multiplayer and live-service — a partner, not a body shop." · "Will it hold up under load?" · "Don't give me a tech demo." · "60 fps, no seams." · "Who owns the netcode?"

## Positioning
- **Differentiation:** Immersive craft *and* systems engineering in one company. Brand B treats an experience or a multiplayer game as a system that must perform, scale, and survive real users — and owns the hard parts (netcode, server fleets, live-service, performance) end to end, not just the visuals.
- **Positioning Statement:** Brand B is the real-time-3D company that builds immersive experiences and multiplayer games like engineered systems — one partner combining attraction-grade craft, AAA multiplayer engineering, and the rigor to make it ship and scale.
- **Competitors:** Enludio (immersive/RT-3D craft; light on AI/software rigor & security); Side / PTW (scale game services, execution-led); Companion Group (senior multiplayer/backend — the premium specialist to beat on engineering); Devoted / Pingle (co-dev capacity); Theory / Talon (Orlando immersive). *(See `../COMPETITIVE_LANDSCAPE.md` §1–2.)*
- **Market Position:** Premium build partner — craft-led and systems-deep. Competes on the fusion (craft + engineering), not on price or pure capacity.

## Brand Voice
**Headline:** *The voice of someone who has shipped worlds.*

**Voice Qualities:**
1. **Vivid** — paints the experience, then proves it with a number.
2. **Exact under the magic** — names the engine, the frame budget, the player count, the latency.
3. **Confident, not loud** — lets the reel and the build carry it; states, doesn't oversell.
4. **Player/guest-first** — centers what the audience feels and does, not the toolchain.
5. **Honest about the hard parts** — netcode, scale, and live-ops are where projects die; says so.

## Values & Mission
**Core Values:**
1. **Craft you can feel** — fidelity, performance, and play are the product.
2. **Built like systems** — real-time performance, netcode, reliability — not just visuals.
3. **Multiplayer-grade engineering** — matchmaking, server fleets, live-service, anti-cheat — owned.
4. **Partnership** — embed with the client's team; own the hard parts.
5. **Ship it** — an experience that breaks on the floor isn't an experience.

**Vision:** To be the company called when an experience has to be both unforgettable *and* unbreakable — immersive craft held to real engineering standards.

**Mission:** Design and engineer real-time-3D worlds, simulations, and multiplayer games that ship on time, scale under load, and feel alive — for studios and operators who can't accept a tech demo.
