## 01 — IDENTITY STRATEGY STATEMENT

Brand B's identity is cinematic, vivid, and engineered. Where the group's software brand is restrained and premium, Brand B is *alive* — depth, light, and motion — but every effect is intentional, the way a great render is. The system should feel like real-time 3D itself: dimensional, lit with purpose, in motion. Spectacle with craft, never noise. It must look as good on a game splash screen and an attraction wall as in a technical deck — because Brand B sells both the wonder and the engineering underneath it.

---

## 02 — LOGO DIRECTION

**Primary direction:** A confident, contemporary mark with a sense of depth or dimension — a wordmark with a structural/spatial device (parallax layering, an isometric or volumetric cue) rather than a flat tech logotype.

**Character:** Dimensional, kinetic, precise. It should imply space and light — like a frame from a real-time scene — without tipping into gimmick.

**Style notes:**
- Constructed and exact, but with depth: layering, an implied light source, or a volumetric/isometric cue.
- Designed to animate — the mark should have a natural motion behaviour (parallax, assembly, light sweep) for splash screens and stings.
- Works on a game splash, an attraction wall, a booth, and a favicon.

**What to avoid:** flat generic tech logotypes; literal gamepad/joystick clichés; "metaverse" cube/orbit motifs; rainbow gradients with no light logic.

**3 Logo References (borrow one quality from each):**
- **Epic Games / Unreal** — borrow the *credibility and weight* of a real-time-engine mark in the games/real-time world.
- **A24** — borrow the *confident, cinematic restraint* of a mark that signals taste and craft, not loudness.
- **Riot Games** — borrow the *energy and play* that reads instantly to a games audience, kept disciplined.

## 03 — COLOR PALETTE

A deep cinematic dark lit by a controlled spectral accent — color used as *real-time light*, not decoration.

**Base**
- **Void** — `#0B0D14` (deep indigo-black). The ground. Cinematic and dimensional — deliberately cooler than the software brand's warm obsidian.

**Signature Accent — Spectral / Iridescent**
- **Electric Cyan** `#2FE6E0` → **Ultraviolet** `#7B5CFF` → **Magenta** `#FF3DAE`. Used as light and energy — gradients, glows, edge light — to create depth.

**Neutrals**
- Cool grays `#161922` / `#222736` (surfaces, panels); off-white `#EAF0FF` (text).

**Usage rules:**
- The spectrum reads as *real-time light*, not a rainbow — directional, motivated, used to model depth and edges.
- More color than the software brand allows — still disciplined. Don't flood; let the spectrum pick out form.
- Keep text legible: off-white on Void; reserve the spectral range for light, accents, and motion.

**Palette type:** Dark base + spectral/iridescent accent range (analogous-to-triadic light spectrum).

## 04 — TYPOGRAPHY

- **Display / brand:** a characterful contemporary sans or techno-display — a wide geometric or a precise techno face with presence on a title card (more expressive than a high-contrast serif). *(e.g. a techno/grotesque display; confirm licensing.)*
- **Body / UI:** a clean neutral sans — **Inter** (or similar) — for documentation and interface.
- **Mono:** a monospace (e.g. **JetBrains Mono**) for build specs and technical credibility.

**Type personality:** contemporary, kinetic, technical — the typographic equivalent of a precise render. Expressive in display, neutral and legible in body.

**Avoid:** rounded/friendly faces; ornate serifs (that's the software brand's register); more than one display voice.

---

## 05 — IMAGERY & MOTION

**Aesthetic:** Real-time-3D worlds, in-engine captures, volumetric light, depth-of-field, motion. Show *the world and the player/guest in it* — not abstract tech.

**Subject matter:** in-engine scenes, the guest/player experiencing the world, behind-the-build captures, performance/scale shown visually.

**Motion is a first-class brand asset:** loops, parallax, real-time captures, light sweeps. The brand should move, because the product does.

**What to avoid:** static stock photography, flat vector illustration, fake "holographic UI" renders, anything that can't be backed by a real build.

---

## 06 — ICONOGRAPHY & DESIGN PRINCIPLES

**Iconography:** dimensional and slightly more playful than the software brand's, but consistent and grid-based; spectral accent used as light/active state.

**Design principles:**
- **Depth over flatness** — imply space and light; the brand lives in 3D.
- **Light with intent** — every glow is motivated, like a render's lighting.
- **Show the world** — lead with the experience and the player/guest in it.
- **Spectacle earned by craft** — wonder backed by engineering, never noise.
- **Motion is brand** — if it can move, it should.

## 07 — BRAND EXPRESSIONS

**Website** — reel-first, with live real-time captures; the world loads like a scene. Void ground, spectral light, motion throughout.

**Social** — behind-the-build clips, in-engine captures, technical-creative threads; motion-native.

**Expo / booth** — bold dimensional presence with live, playable demos; the booth is a render you can walk into.

**Splash / title treatments** — animated logo and key art for game/experience launches.

**Tooling / product UI (Phase 2)** — a pro-creative-tool UI for owned engine plugins/SDKs: Void-dark, spectral accents for state, mono for specs — feels like a tool a senior dev wants open all day.
