---

## 01 — WHAT IS BRAND C?
> *"Immersive realism, held to mission-critical security."*

Brand C builds **secure simulation, training systems, and mission software** for defense and
government — work where failure has real-world cost and the bar for security is absolute. It draws
on the group's real-time-3D and AI core (the same engineering behind Brand B) but operates as a
separate, security-cleared entity, because government contracting and commercial work cannot share
one house. The premise that makes it credible: defense buyers are forced to choose between
immersive studios that lack security rigor and primes that lack immersive/AI craft. Brand C is
built to be both.

---

## 02 — VISION
*To be the partner defense and government call when a training or simulation system has to be as
realistic as it is secure — frontier immersive craft held to mission-critical standards.*

## 03 — MISSION
*Engineer secure, realistic simulation and mission software that people can train and operate on —
built to defense-grade security and compliance from the first commit.*

## 04 — VALUES
- **Security & compliance by default** — architecture decisions, never afterthoughts.
- **Mission reliability** — it has to work when it matters; readiness is the product.
- **Immersive realism** — training value comes from fidelity people trust.
- **Trusted partnership** — a credible long-term vendor through exacting procurement.
- **Process rigor** — the discipline defense requires, matched to genuine craft.

## 05 — TARGET AUDIENCE
**Primary buyers:** Program / Acquisition Directors, simulation & training PMs, defense/government
innovation leads.

**Persona — Colonel R., 52, Program Director, defense simulation & training.** *"I'm not buying a
tech demo. I'm buying a system my people will train on, that meets every requirement, with a
partner I can trust."* Needs defense-grade security and process **plus** real XR/AI/sim capability —
rare in one vendor.

## 06 — PERSONALITY
**Axis:** Serious · Formal · Mission-led · Trustworthy. Sober and exact; gravitas without
theatrics. No consumer-games tone — this brand must read as a defense partner, which is precisely
why it is separated from Brand B.

## 07 — VOICE *(own voice — formal, mission-led)*
Calm authority for risk-averse government buyers; compliance-aware; precise about requirements,
security, and trade-offs. Plain, credible, never flashy. Substance and proof over narrative.

## 08 — COMPETITORS *(see `COMPETITIVE_LANDSCAPE.md` §3)*
GovCIO (federal IT modernization — trust/clearances/contract vehicles, but not immersive); Raytheon
M&S, Calian, HAVELSAN (defense sim primes); XR Training / Operator XR (XR-defense specialists);
Improbable Defence / Anduril (software-first defense). Two competitor types: GovCon primes (trust)
and immersive-sim specialists (craft).

## 09 — POSITIONING
**Statement:** *Brand C is the defense and government partner that builds immersive simulation and
mission software to mission-critical security — combining genuine XR/AI craft with the clearances,
compliance, and process the mission demands.*

**Map:** X = security/compliance rigor ←→ immersive/AI craft; Y = platform/incumbent trust ←→
frontier capability. Brand C aims for the empty top-right: both rigor and craft — the overlap the
Orlando immersive consortium is approaching but lacks the security/AI-engineering depth to own.

## 10 — HOW BRAND C STANDS OUT
Immersive studios lack security and compliance maturity; defense primes lack immersive/AI craft;
IT integrators lack both. Brand C is built to span them — but only earns the claim once it holds
the credentials. Its advantage is structural: a separate, cleared entity drawing on a shared elite
engineering core, so it brings frontier capability *and* mission-grade discipline.

## 11 — TAGLINE DIRECTIONS
- "Trained to the highest standard." *(lead)*
- "Realism, secured."
- "Mission-grade simulation."
- "Built for work too important to get wrong."

## 12 — PROOF POSTURE & ACTIVATION GATE (critical)
This is the brand where over-claiming is most fatal. **Activate only when all three exist:**
(1) the security posture / certifications / clearances the work requires, (2) a real
contract or referenceable program, (3) cleared staff and compliant infrastructure. Until then,
defense work is an internal practice incubated under the group — *not* a public brand. Never
publish defense capability, clearances, or compliance claims that aren't demonstrably true; in
this segment that is the fastest way to lose all credibility.
