## Audience
- **Primary Audience**: Program / Acquisition Directors, simulation & training program managers, defense and government innovation leads. Multi-stakeholder, reference- and compliance-driven, long procurement cycles.
- **Buyer Titles**: Program/Acquisition Director, Simulation & Training Program Manager, Defense/Government Innovation Lead, Contracting Officer (and their technical and security evaluators). _(to confirm by program)_
- **Key Problem**: Defense buyers are forced to choose between immersive studios that lack security rigor and primes that lack immersive/AI craft. They need defense-grade security and process **plus** genuine XR/AI/simulation capability — rare in one vendor.
- **Their Language**: "I'm not buying a tech demo." · "meets every requirement" · "a partner I can trust" · "the safest bid, not the cheapest" · "does it pass accreditation / security review" · "is it referenceable." _(to confirm from real program interviews)_

## Positioning
- **Differentiation**: A separate, cleared entity drawing on a shared elite engineering core — it brings frontier immersive/AI capability *and* mission-grade discipline. Immersive studios lack security/compliance maturity; defense primes lack immersive/AI craft; IT integrators lack both. Brand C is built to span them — but only earns the claim once it holds the credentials.
- **Positioning Statement**: Brand C is the defense and government partner that builds immersive simulation and mission software to mission-critical security — combining genuine XR/AI craft with the clearances, compliance, and process the mission demands.
- **Competitors**: GovCIO (federal IT modernization — trust / contract vehicles, not immersive); Raytheon M&S, Calian, HAVELSAN (simulation primes); XR Training / Operator XR (XR-defense specialists); Improbable Defence / Anduril (software-first defense). Two camps: GovCon primes (trust) and immersive-sim specialists (craft). _(see `../COMPETITIVE_LANDSCAPE.md` §3)_
- **Market Position**: The empty **top-right** of the map — both security/compliance rigor *and* immersive/AI craft; both incumbent-grade trust *and* frontier capability. The overlap the Orlando immersive consortium approaches but lacks the security/AI-engineering depth to own.

## Values & Mission
- **Core Values**:
  1. **Security & compliance by default** — architecture decisions, never afterthoughts.
  2. **Mission reliability** — it works when it matters; readiness is the product.
  3. **Immersive realism** — training value comes from fidelity people trust.
  4. **Trusted partnership** — a credible long-term vendor through exacting procurement.
  5. **Process rigor** — the discipline defense requires, matched to genuine craft.
- **Vision**: To be the partner defense and government call when a training or simulation system must be as realistic as it is secure — frontier immersive craft held to mission-critical standards.
- **Mission**: Engineer secure, realistic simulation and mission software people can train and operate on — built to defense-grade security and compliance from the first commit.

## Goals
- Achieve the security posture, certifications, and clearances to be a credible bidder.
- Win and deliver a first referenceable program.
- Build cleared staff and compliant infrastructure.
- Convert the group's immersive/AI craft into a defensible defense-sim differentiator.
- **Primary Goal**: Clear the Activation Gate, then win and reference a first program. _(metrics to confirm — certifications held, contract value, cleared headcount)_

## Activation Gate *(critical — read before any public use)*
Go public **only when ALL THREE exist**: (1) the security posture / certifications / clearances the work requires; (2) a real contract or referenceable program; (3) cleared staff and compliant infrastructure. Until then, defense work is an internal practice under the group — never a public brand. Never publish defense capability, clearances, or compliance claims that aren't demonstrably true; in this segment that is the fastest way to lose all credibility. _(full gate: `BRAND_STRATEGY.md` §12 and the brief's Part 8)_
