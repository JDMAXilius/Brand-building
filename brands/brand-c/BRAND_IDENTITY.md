# Brand C — Visual Identity Brief

> *Defense & Government (name TBD).* Derives from `brands/brand-c.md` / the brief.
> Part of the silent **Elyoun Group**. **Gated/forward-looking.** Sober and institutional —
> deliberately distinct from the software brand (no gold) and the games brand (no spectrum).
> Items marked **(to confirm)** are inferred.

---

## 01 — IDENTITY STRATEGY STATEMENT

Brand C's identity is trustworthy, precise, and mission-serious. It must read in the visual register of a defense partner: restrained, institutional, and exact — credibility over flair. Every choice serves legibility in a procurement room and a contract PDF, not impact on a feed. Where the group's software brand is premium-dark and the games studio is cinematic-vivid, Brand C is sober and dependable. The restraint is deliberate: in this segment, seriousness signals competence, and flash signals risk.

---

## 02 — LOGO DIRECTION

**Primary direction:** A solid, stable wordmark — engineered and sober — possibly with a shield, precision, or navigation cue. Never a games or consumer feel.

**Character:** Dependable, institutional, exact. Reads as trustworthy at small sizes and on official documents.

**Style notes:** Constructed and grounded; balanced weight; no decorative flourish. Must hold up in monochrome, on a document header, and on a capability statement cover.

**What to avoid:** the games studio's depth/spectrum cues; metallic gold (that's the software brand); any consumer, playful, or hype aesthetic; literal weapons imagery.

**3 Logo References (borrow one quality from each):**
- **Palantir** — gravity and trust in the defense/enterprise space, without decoration.
- **Anduril** — modern-but-serious defense posture; credible to a program office.
- **Lockheed Martin / RAND (institutional marks)** — the *steadiness and legibility* of an established institution on official documents.

---

## 03 — COLOR PALETTE

A deep institutional base with a single restrained, muted accent — and a strong light mode, because procurement reads on paper.

**Base**
- **Graphite** `#12151A` / **Steel Navy** `#0E1B2A` — deep, sober institutional darks.

**Accent (restrained, muted)**
- **Signal Steel-Blue** `#3E6E8E` *or* a desaturated **Tactical Green** `#5A7D5A` — used sparingly for emphasis, never decoration.

**Neutrals**
- Steel grays `#2A2F38`; light `#E6E9ED`.

**Strong light mode (primary for documents):** white/bone ground, dark text, muted accent — built for print-legible procurement materials.

**Usage rules:** No vivid spectrum (that's the games brand). No metallic gold (that's the software brand). Muted, secure, sober. The accent emphasizes; it never decorates.

**Palette type:** Institutional dark + single muted accent, with a dominant light mode for documents.

---

## 04 — TYPOGRAPHY

- **Headings:** a serious, legible sans or a sober slab/serif with institutional gravitas. *(confirm specific face)*
- **Body:** a highly legible workhorse sans — **Inter** (or similar) — optimized for long documents.
- **Mono:** a monospace for technical/spec content.

**Type personality:** institutional, exact, document-first. Legibility and gravitas over expression.

**Avoid:** expressive/techno display faces (games brand); high-contrast fashion serifs (software brand); anything that reads playful.

---

## 05 — IMAGERY

**Aesthetic:** Restrained, credible, mission-context — training environments, operators, secure facilities — used carefully and compliantly. Documentary, not cinematic-flashy.

**What to avoid:** consumer-games aesthetics, hype renders, spectacle, anything that can't be shown compliantly or isn't real.

---

## 06 — ICONOGRAPHY & DESIGN PRINCIPLES

**Iconography:** precise, utilitarian line icons; muted accent only. Prefer diagrams, requirement matrices, and architecture/security diagrams over illustration.

**Design principles:**
- **Credibility over flair** — seriousness is the aesthetic.
- **Clarity for procurement** — built to be read, filed, and evaluated.
- **Restraint signals seriousness** — the quiet option is the right one.
- **Prove, don't promise** — show held credentials and delivered programs, nothing aspirational.

---

## 07 — BRAND EXPRESSIONS

- **Capability statements & white papers** — formal, structured, requirement-led; the primary surface.
- **Documents** — print-first / light-mode; legible on paper and in a contract PDF.
- **Web presence** — sober and minimal; institutional, no spectacle.
- **Demonstration materials** — compliant settings only; evidence-led.
- *No social spectacle or expo theatrics in the games sense.*

---

## 08 — NAMING & ACTIVATION GATE

**Naming brief** *(name TBD — see `../NAMING.md`)*: sober, secure, mission-led, procurement-legible, no games vibe. Current lead: **Redoubt** (a fortification; clean in the defense space). Runner-up: **Warden**. (Bulwark / Aegis / Sentinel dropped — taken/overused in defense.)

**ACTIVATION GATE (critical — read before any public use).** This is the brand where over-claiming is most fatal. **Go public only when ALL THREE exist:**
1. the security posture / certifications / clearances the work requires,
2. a real contract or referenceable program,
3. cleared staff and compliant infrastructure.

Until then, defense work is an **internal practice** incubated under the group — *not* a public brand. Never publish defense capability, clearances, or compliance claims that aren't demonstrably true; in this segment that is the fastest way to lose all credibility.
