# Brand C — Complete Brand Definition
### Defense & Government *(name TBD — future, gated)*

> The complete brand book for Brand C — one of three independent companies under the silent Elyoun
> Group (`../BRAND_ARCHITECTURE.md`). **Forward-looking blueprint.** Brand C is stood up LAST and
> only once clearances / certifications / contracts exist. Ring-fenced for procurement and security.
> Its system is sober and mission-led — deliberately unlike the games brand. Generated 2026-06-28.

---

## PART 1 — STRATEGY

### 1.1 Essence
> *"Immersive realism, held to mission-critical security."*

Brand C builds **secure simulation, training systems, and mission software** for defense and
government — work where failure has real-world cost and the security bar is absolute. It draws on
the group's real-time-3D and AI core (the engineering behind Brand B) but operates as a separate,
security-cleared entity, because government contracting and commercial work cannot share one house.
Its reason to exist: defense buyers are forced to choose between immersive studios that lack
security rigor and primes that lack immersive/AI craft. Brand C is built to be both.

### 1.2 Vision
To be the partner defense and government call when a training or simulation system must be as
realistic as it is secure — frontier immersive craft held to mission-critical standards.

### 1.3 Mission
Engineer secure, realistic simulation and mission software people can train and operate on — built
to defense-grade security and compliance from the first commit.

### 1.4 Values
- **Security & compliance by default** — architecture decisions, never afterthoughts.
- **Mission reliability** — it works when it matters; readiness is the product.
- **Immersive realism** — training value comes from fidelity people trust.
- **Trusted partnership** — a credible long-term vendor through exacting procurement.
- **Process rigor** — the discipline defense requires, matched to genuine craft.

### 1.5 Goals
- Achieve the security posture, certifications, and clearances to be a credible bidder.
- Win and deliver a first referenceable program.
- Build cleared staff and compliant infrastructure.
- Convert the group's immersive/AI craft into a defensible defense-sim differentiator.

## PART 4 — PERSONALITY & VOICE

### 4.1 Personality
**Axis:** Serious · Formal · Mission-led · Trustworthy. Sober and exact; gravitas without theatrics.
No consumer-games tone — which is exactly why Brand C is separated from Brand B.

### 4.2 Voice essence
*Brand C sounds like a trusted defense engineer — calm, precise, compliance-aware, and completely
without flash. Authority comes from rigor and proof.*

### 4.3 Voice qualities (with do / don't)
- **Mission-led** — *Do:* "Built for the readiness outcome your program is measured on." *Don't:*
  "A game-changing platform for the warfighter."
- **Security-aware** — speak to compliance, accreditation, and data handling plainly and correctly.
- **Precise** — exact about requirements, capabilities, and limits; no hand-waving.
- **Trustworthy** — explicit about trade-offs, risks, timelines; honesty is the trust signal.
- **Restrained** — no consumer/games tone, no hype, no spectacle for its own sake.

### 4.4 Tone dimensions
Formality: high (fuller forms acceptable in formal/contract contexts). Energy: calm. Expertise:
authoritative and compliance-fluent. Warmth: professional, dependable. Humor: effectively none in
brand/sales — stakes are too high.

### 4.5 Vocabulary
**Use:** mission · readiness · simulation · training · secure · compliance · accreditation ·
requirements · reliability · fidelity · classified/controlled (as appropriate) · program ·
trusted · production. **Avoid:** game-changing · revolutionary · "metaverse" · "cool" · any
consumer-games framing · unprovable capability or security claims.

### 4.6 Style rules
Formal, precise, complete sentences. Trade-offs and limits stated explicitly. No exclamation marks.
Numerals for measurable claims. Active voice (passive only where the actor is genuinely irrelevant).
Light-mode, print-legible documents for procurement.

### 4.7 Before / after
- *Off:* "Our game-changing XR platform is a paradigm shift for defense." → *On:* "Immersive
  simulation built to [accreditation] standards — realism your people can train on, security your
  program can certify."

### 5.5 What the work requires *(procurement reality, not marketing proof)*
> Defense is the one exception to the group-wide "don't lean on proof" rule — **not** because Brand
> C name-drops clients (it never does), but because certifications, clearances, and past performance
> are *scored bid requirements* in government procurement. These are credentials to operate, not
> testimonials to impress with.
- Security posture / certifications / accreditations actually held.
- Cleared staff, compliant infrastructure, and referenceable program performance (procurement-mandated).
- Immersive/AI/simulation capability demonstrable in a compliant context.
- No commercial-style logos, reviews, or "trusted by" marketing — the work and the credentials stand.

### 5.6 By channel
Capability statements and white papers (formal). Program references and demonstrations (compliant
settings). Industry-day and procurement materials. No consumer/social spectacle.

## PART 6 — VISUAL IDENTITY *(sober, institutional — distinct from A and B)*

### 6.1 Identity statement
Trustworthy, precise, mission-serious. The visual register of a defense partner: restrained,
institutional, and exact. Credibility over flair; legible in a procurement room and a contract PDF.

### 6.2 Logo
A solid, stable wordmark — engineered and sober, possibly with a shield/precision/navigation cue,
never a games or consumer feel. Reads as dependable at small sizes and on official documents.

### 6.3 Color
- **Base:** deep institutional dark — Graphite `#12151A` / Steel Navy `#0E1B2A`.
- **Accent:** restrained and muted — Signal Steel-Blue `#3E6E8E` or a desaturated tactical
  green `#5A7D5A`; used sparingly for emphasis, never decoration.
- Neutrals: steel grays `#2A2F38`, light `#E6E9ED`. **Strong light-mode** for documents (white/
  bone ground, dark text) — procurement reads on paper.
- Rule: no vivid spectrum (that's Brand B), no metallic gold (that's Brand A). Muted, secure, sober.

### 6.4 Typography
- **Headings:** a serious, legible sans or a sober slab/serif with institutional gravitas.
- **Body:** a highly legible workhorse sans (Inter or similar), optimized for long documents.
- **Mono:** for technical/spec content.

### 6.5 Imagery
Restrained, credible, mission-context (training environments, operators, secure facilities) — used
carefully and compliantly. Documentary, not cinematic-flashy. No consumer-games aesthetics, no
hype renders.

### 6.6 Iconography & principles
Precise, utilitarian line icons; muted accent only. Prefer diagrams, requirement matrices, and
architecture/security diagrams. Principles: credibility over flair · clarity for procurement ·
restraint signals seriousness · prove, don't promise.

### 6.7 Expressions
Formal capability statements and white papers; print-first/light-mode documents; sober web presence;
compliant demonstration materials. No social spectacle or expo theatrics in the games sense.

## PART 8 — POSTURE & ACTIVATION GATE

**Posture — an established, cleared, expert organization.** Brand C speaks as a capable defense
partner with senior, multi-disciplinary teams — never a newcomer, never a single person. Authority
comes from rigor, credentials, and the work; it does not name-drop or lean on commercial-style
social proof.

**Activation gate (critical — read before any public use).** This is the brand where over-claiming
is most fatal. **Go public only when ALL THREE exist:**
1. the security posture / certifications / clearances the work requires,
2. a real contract or referenceable program,
3. cleared staff and compliant infrastructure.

Until then, defense work is an **internal practice** incubated under the group — *not* a public
brand. Never publish defense capability, clearances, or compliance claims that aren't demonstrably
true; in this segment that is the fastest way to lose all credibility.
