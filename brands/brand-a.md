## PART 1 — STRATEGY

### 1.1 Essence
> *"Production-grade AI and software — built like we own it."*

Brand A is a premium AI and software engineering company that **builds and operates its own
software products** *and* **engineers software for clients** who need elite capability they can't
staff in-house. Because it runs its own products in production, it brings product-builder
discipline and full ownership to client work — it knows what it costs to run a system, not just
demo one.

### 1.2 Vision
To be the engineering partner ambitious companies call when software is too important to get wrong
— built on senior craft, applied AI, and a refusal to ship anything less than production-grade.

### 1.3 Mission
Engineer the AI-powered software clients can't build alone — from a founder's first product to an
enterprise's hardest integration — and stand behind every system in production.

### 1.4 Values
- **Engineering excellence** — production-grade or it doesn't ship.
- **Ownership** — embed, own outcomes, tell the truth (including when an idea should change).
- **Applied AI** — AI where it earns its place in production, never as a buzzword.
- **Security by default** — real security standards from the first commit.
- **Craft** — quality in the parts no one sees as much as the parts they do.

### 1.5 Goals
- Establish Brand A as the premium "ships-its-own-products" engineering partner.
- Convert the in-production product into the lead proof and first reference.
- Land a small set of marquee client engagements (one founder-stage, one enterprise) as flagships.
- Grow product + services so each compounds the other.

Technical founders/CEOs (seed–Series B) and enterprise CTO / CIO / Chief Innovation Officers, in

too stretched to build it all. *"I need a team that's actually built and run a product like the one

### 2.3 What they care about
Maya: ship a credible v1 fast without losing control/equity; de-risk the technical story for
investors. David: move AI from pilot to production; pass security/compliance; show outcomes.

## PART 4 — PERSONALITY & VOICE

### 4.1 Personality
**Axis:** Serious · Premium · Precise · Calm-authority. The senior engineer who has shipped the
hard thing and doesn't need to shout about it. Premium without being precious; bold in ambition,
disciplined in claim.

### 4.2 Voice essence
*Brand A sounds like the expert who doesn't need to impress you — precise, confident, and free of
hype.* (Inherits the existing Elyoun voice; keep intact.)

### 4.3 Voice qualities (with do / don't)
- **Precise** — *Do:* "Cut onboarding from 9 steps to 3." *Don't:* "Dramatically improved the UX."
- **Confident, not loud** — *Do:* "In production with three enterprise clients." *Don't:*
  "Best-in-class platform taking the industry by storm."
- **Hype-free** — *Do:* "Applied AI, built to your security requirements." *Don't:* "Revolutionary,
  world-class, next-gen AI."
- **Technical when it counts** — architecture detail for a CTO, business outcome for a CIO; never
  talk down or show off.
- **Outcome-oriented** — *Do:* "You get a system in production, owned end to end." *Don't:* "Our
  proprietary 7-phase framework."
- **Trustworthy** — *Do:* "Faster, but costs more to maintain. Here's the trade." *Don't:* hide
  the caveats.
- **Elevated** — tight, considered, error-free; the writing is a sample of the craft.

### 4.4 Tone dimensions
Formality: formal-leaning, never stiff. Energy: calm authority. Expertise: authoritative, flexes
to accessible. Warmth: professionally warm — a stakeholder, not a faceless firm. Humor: serious,
with rare dry understatement.

### 4.5 Vocabulary
**Use:** build · ship · production-grade · own/ownership · secure · security-by-default · partner ·
applied · craft · standard · in production · outcomes · trade-off · architecture · senior ·
embedded. **Avoid:** revolutionary · world-class · cutting-edge · game-changing · synergy ·
leverage (verb) · disrupt · seamless · best-in-class · solutioning · cheap/budget · paradigm.

### 4.6 Style rules
Short-to-medium sentences, lead with the point. Em-dashes for precision. **No exclamation marks**
in brand/sales copy. Sentence case. Numerals for anything measurable (3 weeks, 40 ms). Contractions
on. Active voice.

### 4.7 Before / after
- *Off:* "We leverage cutting-edge AI to deliver revolutionary solutions." → *On:* "We build
  production-grade AI software — and we run our own products, so we build yours like owners."

## PART 5 — MESSAGING

### 5.1 Core message
*Brand A turns ambitious companies' hardest software ideas into production-grade AI systems — by
building like owners, not vendors.*

### 5.2 Value proposition
A premium AI & software engineering company that consults *and* ships its own products — so it
brings product-builder discipline and full ownership to client work, to a standard that satisfies
a founder and an enterprise security review alike.

### 5.3 Taglines
- **"Built like owners."** *(lead)*
- "Production-grade AI & software."
- "We ship our own. We'll build yours like we own it."
- "Software, to the highest standard."

### 5.4 Messaging hierarchy
- **L1 headline:** Built like owners.
- **L2 supporting:** Brand A builds the AI software the most ambitious companies can't build alone
  — and runs its own products, so it builds yours like an owner.
- **L3 key messages:** (1) *We build like owners, not vendors.* (2) *Production-grade, or it doesn't
  ship.* (3) *Senior people, owned outcomes.* (4) *Applied AI that survives security review.*

### 5.5 How we demonstrate capability *(the work, not social proof)*
- The software product(s) the company builds and runs in production — show the product.
- Security-by-default architecture and the engineering itself.
- A senior, expert team; no junior hand-offs.
- **No** client logos, testimonials, reviews, or "trusted by" lists — the work speaks for itself.

### 5.6 Audience-specific
- **Founders:** lead with "builders, not billers"; avoid heavy enterprise/process language.
- **Enterprise:** lead with "AI that ships to production and passes security review"; avoid hype.

### 5.7 By channel
Website hero: visionary, spare. Social (LinkedIn): authoritative, insight-led. Email: direct,
outcome-first. Proposals: formal, trade-offs explicit. Ads: one sharp claim + proof.

## PART 6 — VISUAL IDENTITY

### 6.1 Identity statement
Elite, precise, quietly certain — the visual equivalent of the senior engineer who has shipped the
hard thing. Confidence through reduction: generous dark space, one disciplined accent, sharp type.

### 6.2 Logo
Wordmark-led, high-contrast serif, with a derived monogram for compact use; a single gold full-stop
device as the signature ("the standard, full stop"). Constructed, optically corrected, restrained.
Avoid geometric-sans tech clichés and literal AI motifs (nodes, circuits, gradients-on-a-cube).

### 6.3 Color *(inherits Elyoun system)*
- **Obsidian** `#0A0A0C` (ground) · **Elyoun Gold** `#C8A96A` / light `#E3C98C` (accent, ≤10%)
- Neutrals: Surface `#15151B`, Hairline `#2A2A31`, Bone text `#ECE8DF`, Muted `#9C988C`, Slate
  `#6B7A8F` (data only). Light-mode inverse for docs. No pure black/white.

### 6.4 Typography
Display/brand: **Playfair Display** (high-contrast serif). Headings/UI: **Space Grotesk**
(precise geometric sans). Body: **Inter**. Optional **JetBrains Mono** for code/technical.

### 6.5 Imagery
Cinematic, low-key, precise. The work/artifacts (code on dark screens, interfaces lit in the dark),
people in real concentration, abstract gold-lit craft. Dark desaturated base, warm gold highlights.
No bright corporate stock, no blue-tech clichés.

### 6.6 Iconography & principles
Thin sharp-cornered line icons on a grid; gold for active state only. Prefer diagrams over
illustration. Principles: confidence through reduction · the accent is earned · precision in every
detail · elevated, not decorative.

### 6.7 Expressions
Dark-first website; gold-foil black stationery; obsidian decks (≤10% gold); light-mode proposals;
dark-first product UI (gold for primary actions only).

## PART 8 — POSTURE (established expert firm)
Brand A speaks as a capable company of senior experts — not a newcomer proving itself, and never a
single freelancer ("we," the team, the firm). Show the product it builds and runs in production —
the work is the demonstration. State capability plainly and let it stand. **No** client logos,
testimonials, reviews, "trusted by" lists, invented metrics, or aspirational certs — modern buyers
judge the work, and name-dropping reads as insecurity.
