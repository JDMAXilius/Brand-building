# Elyoun — Brand Voice & Tone Guide

> Verbal identity. Built on `.agents/brand-context.md` and `BRAND_STRATEGY.md`.
> Generated 2026-06-27. Voice stays constant; tone flexes by channel.

---

## 01 — VOICE OVERVIEW

Elyoun sounds like the senior engineer who has actually shipped the hard thing — quietly certain, precise, and completely uninterested in hype. It writes the way its best people talk: plainly, specifically, with the confidence of having done the work rather than the need to impress. Because Elyoun's buyers range from technical founders to enterprise executives to defense program directors, the voice earns trust through substance — concrete nouns, real numbers, honest trade-offs — not through volume or adjectives. It is premium without being precious, formal where credibility demands it, and direct everywhere. The whole brand rests on doing exactly what it says, so the voice never promises what the work can't back.

**Voice Essence:** *Elyoun sounds like the expert who doesn't need to impress you — precise, confident, and free of hype.*

---

## 02 — TONE DIMENSIONS

**Formality** — Casual ←———————●——→ Formal
- Sits on the formal-leaning side, but never stiff or corporate. Polished and considered, like a senior consultant who respects your time.
- In practice: clean, complete sentences; no slang; no bureaucratic padding either.
- *Example:* "We'll have a working prototype in your hands in three weeks, and we'll tell you what it can't yet do."

**Energy** — Calm ←——●———————→ Energetic
- Calm authority. Elyoun states; it doesn't shout. Confidence comes from certainty, not exclamation.
- In practice: measured pacing, declarative sentences, no manufactured excitement.
- *Example:* "This runs in production today. Here's how it holds up under load."

**Expertise** — Accessible ←————●——●——→ Authoritative
- Authoritative by default, and able to flex toward accessible without dumbing down — deep with technical buyers, clear with executives.
- In practice: go technical when the reader is technical; translate, never condescend, when they're not.
- *Example (exec):* "AI that passes your security review and ships to production." *Example (technical):* "Inference runs at the edge with a 40 ms p99; no data leaves your VPC."

**Warmth** — Distant ←————●———→ Intimate
- Professionally warm. Elyoun is a partner, not a friend and not a faceless firm — invested, candid, human.
- In practice: speak as a stakeholder who owns the outcome; "we" and "you," plainly.
- *Example:* "We'd build this differently than you've scoped it — here's why, and what it saves you."

**Humor** — Serious ←——●————————→ Witty
- Serious, with rare dry understatement. Stakes are high for Elyoun's buyers (enterprise, defense), so wit is sparing and never at the expense of clarity or credibility.
- *Example:* "Most 'AI strategies' die in security review. This one was built for it."

## 03 — VOICE QUALITIES

**Precise**
- Elyoun says exactly what it means and quantifies wherever it can. Vagueness reads as a tell that you haven't built the thing.
- **Do:** "Cut onboarding from 9 steps to 3, and time-to-first-value from days to under two minutes."
- **Don't:** "Dramatically improved the user experience and streamlined the flow."

**Confident, not loud**
- Authority comes from substance, not volume. Elyoun makes claims it can stand behind and lets the work carry the weight.
- **Do:** "This is in production with three enterprise clients."
- **Don't:** "Our incredible, best-in-class platform is taking the industry by storm!"

**Hype-free**
- No buzzword inflation. If a claim can't be demonstrated in production, Elyoun doesn't make it.
- **Do:** "Applied AI, built to your security requirements."
- **Don't:** "Revolutionary, world-class, next-generation AI synergy."

**Technical when it counts**
- Elyoun goes deep with technical readers and stays clear with executive ones — never showing off, never talking down.
- **Do:** Match the reader: architecture detail for a CTO, business outcome for a CIO.
- **Don't:** Bury an executive in jargon, or hand an engineer marketing fluff.

**Outcome-oriented**
- The language centers what the client gets — shipped, secure, running — not Elyoun's internal process or methodology.
- **Do:** "You get a system in production, owned end to end."
- **Don't:** "Our proprietary 7-phase agile delivery framework ensures alignment."

**Trustworthy**
- Elyoun is plain about trade-offs, risks, and timelines. Honesty is the premium signal, especially for enterprise and defense buyers.
- **Do:** "This approach is faster but costs more to maintain. Here's the trade."
- **Don't:** Promise everything, hide the caveats, and hope.

**Elevated**
- Every detail is polished, because the writing is itself a sample of the craft Elyoun sells.
- **Do:** Tight, considered, error-free copy.
- **Don't:** Sloppy formatting, typos, or filler — they undercut the whole premium promise.

## 04 — VOCABULARY

**Words we use:**
build · ship · production-grade · own / ownership · secure / security-by-default · partner · engineer · applied · craft · standard · in production · outcomes · scope · trade-off · architecture · immersive · simulation · mission-critical · embedded · senior

**Words we avoid:**
revolutionary · world-class · cutting-edge · game-changing · next-gen (as a boast) · synergy · leverage (as a verb) · disrupt · seamless (overused) · best-in-class · ninja/rockstar/guru · solutioning · cheap / budget · "just" (as in "just a quick build") · paradigm · unlock (as filler) · empower (as filler)

**Brand-specific language:**
- The company is **Elyoun** (from *elyon*, "the highest") — never "the brand," never abbreviated.
- Refer to clients as **clients** or **partners**, not "users" (reserve "users" for the end-users of what Elyoun builds).
- Distinguish a **system** (production-grade, owned) from a **demo/prototype** — the gap between them is core to the brand. Use the distinction deliberately.
- The model is **consulting + our own products** — name both; the duality is the differentiator.

---

## 05 — WRITING STYLE RULES

**Sentence length:** Default to short and medium. Lead with the point. Use an occasional longer sentence to carry nuance or a trade-off, then return to short for emphasis.

**Punctuation:** Em-dashes encouraged for precision and aside. Ellipses rarely. **Exclamation marks effectively banned** in brand and sales copy — they read as the hype Elyoun avoids.

**Capitalization:** Sentence case for headlines and UI. Title case only for proper names. No ALL-CAPS for emphasis.

**Numbers:** Use numerals for anything measurable or technical (3 weeks, 40 ms, 3 clients) — specificity is on-brand. Spell out only at the start of a sentence.

**Contractions:** Use them — they keep the voice human and direct (we'll, it's, you're). The exception is high-formality defense/legal contexts, where fuller forms may suit.

**Active vs passive:** Active voice, always — Elyoun owns its claims and actions. Passive only when the actor is genuinely irrelevant.

**Humor:** Allowed sparingly — dry, intelligent understatement only. Never slapstick, never at a client's expense, never where it muddies a technical or security point.

## 06 — CHANNEL ADAPTATIONS

| Channel | Tone Adjustment | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Website homepage | Sharpest, most visionary; confident and spare | "Engineered to the highest standard. From a founder's first SaaS to defense-grade simulation." |
| Social (LinkedIn) | Authoritative, insight-led; share the engineering point of view | "Most AI pilots die in security review. Here's how we architect so ours don't." |
| Email (outreach/nurture) | Direct, personal, outcome-first; respect the reader's time | "We build and run our own products — so we'll build yours like owners. Worth 20 minutes?" |
| Proposals / sales docs | Formal, precise, trade-offs explicit; certainty + honesty | "Here's the scope, the timeline, and what we'd deliberately leave for phase two." |
| Customer / client support | Warm, accountable, plain; own the issue | "That's on us. Here's the fix and when it lands." |
| Product / UI copy | Minimal, clear, calm; never cute under pressure | "Saved. Your changes are live." / (error) "Couldn't reach the server. Retrying — your work is safe." |

## 07 — BEFORE AND AFTER EXAMPLES

**Before (off-brand):**
> We leverage cutting-edge AI to deliver revolutionary, world-class digital solutions that empower your business to disrupt the market.

**After (on-brand):**
> We build production-grade AI software — and we run our own products, so we build yours like owners.

**Why it works:** Replaces hollow hype with the concrete differentiator and a real promise.

---

**Before (off-brand):**
> Our innovative team is passionate about creating seamless, best-in-class experiences for all our valued clients!

**After (on-brand):**
> Senior engineers who've shipped real products, applied to your hardest problem.

**Why it works:** Trades adjectives and an exclamation mark for substance and specificity.

---

**Before (off-brand):**
> We think we can probably help improve your systems and maybe make things a bit more efficient over time.

**After (on-brand):**
> We can take this to production in three weeks. Here's exactly what ships, and what doesn't yet.

**Why it works:** Confident and precise, with an honest caveat — the trust signal.

---

**Before (off-brand):**
> Unfortunately, we deeply apologize, but it appears there may have been a slight issue on our end.

**After (on-brand):**
> That's on us. Here's the fix and when it lands.

**Why it works:** Accountable and plain, not over-apologetic or evasive.

---

**Before (off-brand):**
> Our game-changing XR simulation platform is a paradigm shift for the entire defense industry.

**After (on-brand):**
> Immersive simulation built to defense-grade security — realism your people can train on.

**Why it works:** Names the real value (realism + security) instead of claiming a "paradigm shift."

## 08 — THINGS WE NEVER SAY

- **Hollow hype:** "world-class," "revolutionary," "game-changing," "best-in-class," "cutting-edge," "next-gen" (as a boast).
- **Buzzword filler:** "leverage," "synergy," "disrupt," "paradigm," "solutioning," "empower/unlock" as padding.
- **Exclamation-mark excitement** in brand or sales copy.
- **Over-apology:** "we deeply apologize for any inconvenience this may have caused" — own it plainly instead.
- **False urgency / pressure:** "act now," "limited time," manufactured scarcity. Elyoun's buyers make considered decisions.
- **Vague hedging:** "probably," "maybe," "a bit," "kind of" — Elyoun is precise or it's quiet.
- **Promises the work can't back:** never claim production-readiness, security, or capability that isn't demonstrably true.
- **Talking down** to technical readers, or **burying** executive readers in jargon.
