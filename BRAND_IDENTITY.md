## 01 — IDENTITY STRATEGY STATEMENT

Elyoun is the premium AI and software engineering partner for work too important to get wrong — a consultancy that ships its own products, serving founders through defense. The identity must read *elite, precise, and quietly certain* — the visual equivalent of the senior engineer who has shipped the hard thing and doesn't need to shout about it. It draws on the meaning of the name itself (*elyon* — "the highest"): elevated, restrained, and exact, with warmth and craft in the details rather than decoration. Every visual decision favors confidence through reduction — generous black space, one disciplined accent, sharp type — so the system feels equally at home in a founder pitch and a defense procurement room.

## 02 — LOGO DIRECTION

**Primary direction:** Wordmark-led, with a derived lettermark ("E") monogram for compact use (favicon, app icon, social avatar). Lead with the wordmark **Elyoun** set in a high-contrast serif; the lettermark is the system's secondary, square-format mark.

**Character:** Elevated, exact, and trustworthy. The mark should feel engineered — confident proportions, nothing arbitrary — with a single moment of distinction (see the period/dot device below) that signals craft.

**Style notes:**
- **Constructed, not hand-drawn.** Precise, optically corrected, tight-but-breathing kerning.
- **High-contrast serif wordmark** (thick/thin stroke modulation) to convey the "elevated/premium" register — this is the brand's signature against a sea of sans-serif tech logos.
- **Signature device:** a gold full-stop after the wordmark — **Elyoun.** — as in the deck. The dot is the only color in the mark; it reads as precision, finality, and "the standard, full stop."
- **Lettermark:** an "E" built on the same serif skeleton, set in a thin gold-ruled square — restrained, like a maker's mark or a hallmark stamp.
- Works in three lockups: full wordmark (primary), wordmark + descriptor ("Elyoun — AI & Software Engineering"), and the E monogram (icon).

**What to avoid:**
- Generic geometric-sans tech wordmarks (the Helvetica/Inter-logo look every dev shop uses).
- Literal AI clichés: neural-net nodes, circuit traces, hexagons, brain/chip motifs, gradients-on-a-cube, "swoosh" orbits.
- Letter-with-a-glowing-gradient, isometric 3D, or anything that looks like a startup-generator output.
- Over-ornate luxury serifs that tip into fashion/jewelry — Elyoun is premium *engineering*, not couture.

**3 Logo References (borrow one quality from each):**
- **The Economist** — borrow the *authoritative high-contrast serif wordmark* used by a serious, premium institution.
- **Palantir** — borrow the *restraint and gravity*: a quiet, confident mark that reads as trusted by defense and enterprise without decoration.
- **Vogue / Dior (serif mastery, not the fashion)** — borrow only the *discipline of an elegant high-contrast serif* — the elevated feel — then strip the luxury-fashion connotation back toward engineering.

## 03 — COLOR PALETTE

A near-monochrome dark system anchored by a single warm metallic accent. The discipline is the point: one accent, used sparingly, against deep neutrals.

**Primary color**
- **Obsidian** — `#0A0A0C`. The brand's ground. A near-black with a faint cool cast — premium, serious, infinite. Signals focus, gravity, and confidence; lets the gold and type carry all the emotion.

**Accent**
- **Elyoun Gold** — `#C8A96A` (core) / `#E3C98C` (light, for text/gradients on dark). Warm metallic gold = "the highest standard," craft, value, and elevation — without the coldness of typical tech blue or the loudness of bright accents. Used *only* for emphasis: the logo dot, key lines, highlights, one or two words per layout.
- Optional gold gradient for display moments: `linear-gradient(135deg, #E3C98C → #C8A96A → #A8884B)`.

**Neutrals (supporting)**
- **Surface** — `#15151B` / `#1B1B22` — elevated panels, cards on the obsidian ground.
- **Hairline** — `#2A2A31` — borders, dividers, table rules.
- **Bone (text)** — `#ECE8DF` — primary text on dark (warm off-white, never pure #FFF).
- **Muted** — `#9C988C` — secondary text, captions.
- **Slate** — `#6B7A8F` — the *only* secondary hue, reserved for data/charts (e.g. competitors on the positioning map) so gold always means "us / emphasis."

**Light-mode inverse (for documents, contracts, some web):**
- Ground **Bone** `#F7F5F0`, text **Obsidian** `#0A0A0C`, accent **Gold** `#A8884B` (slightly deepened for AA contrast on light).

**Color personality:** Elevated, nocturnal, exact. The territory of premium instruments and serious institutions — not playful, not corporate-blue, not luxury-gaudy.

**Usage rules:**
- Gold is a *spice*, not a base — target ≤10% of any composition. If everything is gold, nothing is.
- Never gold text on gold, never gold on slate. Gold on obsidian/bone only.
- Maintain WCAG AA: Elyoun Gold `#C8A96A` on `#0A0A0C` passes for large/UI; use **Light Gold `#E3C98C`** for body-size text on dark.
- Pure black (`#000`) and pure white (`#FFF`) are off-brand — always the warmed neutrals.

**Palette type:** Monochromatic-neutral + single metallic accent (with slate as a constrained data-only secondary).

## 04 — TYPOGRAPHY

A three-role system already proven in the deck: an elegant high-contrast serif for brand moments, a precise geometric sans for structure, and a neutral workhorse for reading.

**Display / Brand — Playfair Display** (Google, free)
High-contrast transitional serif. Carries the "elevated / highest standard" register and is the typographic differentiator against all-sans tech brands. Use for the wordmark feel, hero statements, pull-quotes, and big numbers. Italic for emphasis on key words.

**Headings / UI / Labels — Space Grotesk** (Google, free)
Precise, slightly technical geometric sans. The "engineered" voice — section labels, kickers (letter-spaced uppercase), table headers, buttons, eyebrows. Conveys exactness without coldness.

**Body — Inter** (Google, free)
Neutral, highly legible workhorse for paragraphs, documentation, captions, UI text. Disappears so the content reads as precise and clear.

**Type personality:** Elevated *and* engineered — the serif says "premium institution," the geometric sans says "precision instrument," the body says "clear and credible." Together: authority without hype.

**Usage hierarchy**
| Role | Typeface | Treatment |
|---|---|---|
| Display / Hero | Playfair Display | 600–700, tight leading (~1.05), letter-spacing −1% |
| H1 / H2 | Space Grotesk | 600, sentence case |
| Eyebrow / Label | Space Grotesk | 600, UPPERCASE, letter-spacing +0.26em, gold |
| Body | Inter | 300–400, leading 1.5–1.65 |
| Caption / Meta | Inter | 400, muted |
| Code / Technical | JetBrains Mono *(optional add)* | 400 — for product/eng contexts |

**Avoid:** rounded/friendly sans (Poppins, Nunito, Comic-anything), default system fonts in brand contexts, slab serifs, condensed display faces, and more than one accent typeface. Never set body copy in Playfair.

## 05 — IMAGERY & PHOTOGRAPHY STYLE

**Overall aesthetic:** Cinematic, low-key, and precise. Deep shadows, controlled single-source lighting, a sense of focus and craft. Images should feel *engineered and observed*, never stocky or staged-cheerful.

**Subject matter (in priority order):**
1. **The work / artifacts** — close, tactile detail: code on dark screens, XR/simulation environments, interfaces lit in the dark, hardware, materials. Macro and texture over wide team shots.
2. **People at work** — engineers in concentration, real moments, environmental and candid; never posed "diverse team smiling at laptop."
3. **Abstract craft / light** — gold-lit edges, depth-of-field bokeh, dark architectural space — used as texture behind type.
4. **Domain context** — restrained, credible nods to immersive/defense/enterprise environments.

**Color treatment:** Dark, desaturated base with **warm gold highlights** (single warm light source). Crushed but detailed blacks, no bright whites. A subtle gold/amber grade ties imagery to the palette. High contrast, low key.

**What to avoid:** Bright corporate stock (people high-fiving, glossy open-plan offices), blue-tech clichés (glowing circuits, binary rain, holographic UIs, robot hands), HDR over-production, rainbow gradients, and anything cheerful/flat. If it looks like an AI-startup template, it's wrong.

## 06 — ICONOGRAPHY & ILLUSTRATION

**Style:** Line icons — geometric, sharp-cornered (not rounded), drawn on a consistent grid. Functional and engineered, matching Space Grotesk's character.

**Weight:** Thin-to-regular stroke (~1.5px on a 24px grid), uniform across the set. Gold reserved for an active/emphasis state only; default stroke in Bone or Muted.

**Personality:** Utilitarian and precise — icons are instruments, not decoration. No filled cartoon icons, no duotone, no playful flourishes.

**Illustration / graphic language:** Prefer *data and diagram* over illustration — positioning maps, architecture diagrams, thin gold hairline rules, and quadrant/grid systems (as in the deck). When a graphic device is needed, use precise line geometry and a single gold highlight. Avoid character illustration entirely.

## 07 — DESIGN PRINCIPLES

**Confidence through reduction.** Generous negative space, few elements, one accent. The premium signal is restraint — if a layout feels busy, remove, don't add. Black space is the luxury.

**The accent is earned.** Gold marks only what matters most — one or two moments per composition. Overuse cheapens it; scarcity makes it read as precision and value.

**Precision in every detail.** Optical alignment, consistent hairlines, exact spacing, no orphaned type. The craft of the layout *is* the brand promise — sloppy execution contradicts "production-grade."

**Elevated, not decorative.** Elegance comes from type, contrast, and proportion — never from ornament, effects, or trend. When in doubt, choose the quieter option.

## 08 — BRAND EXPRESSIONS

**Social profiles & posts** — Avatar: gold-ruled **E** monogram on obsidian. Banners: wordmark + one line of gold-accented messaging on black. Post templates: obsidian ground, Space Grotesk eyebrow in gold, a Playfair statement, one idea per post. No bright graphics — the feed should look like a single dark, premium object.

**Website / app** — Dark-first. Obsidian ground, bone text, surfaces at `#15151B`, hairline `#2A2A31`. Playfair hero statements with a single gold word; Space Grotesk nav/labels; Inter body. Sparse gold CTAs. Generous whitespace, cinematic dark imagery, thin-line diagrams. Optional light-mode inverse for docs/legal.

**Business cards / stationery** — Heavy uncoated or soft-touch black stock, **gold foil** wordmark/dot (the foil literalizes "the highest standard"). Minimal: name in Space Grotesk, everything else stripped back. Letterhead: bone paper, obsidian type, a single gold hairline.

**Presentations / pitch decks** — Already defined: obsidian slides, gold accent, Playfair display + Space Grotesk labels + Inter body, gold-on-black data viz with slate as the only secondary. Maintain ≤10% gold per slide. (See `Elyoun-Brand-Strategy.pptx/.pdf/.html`.)

**Proposals & technical docs** — Light-mode inverse for long-form readability: bone ground, obsidian text, deepened gold `#A8884B` for headers/rules. Same type system. Signals the same brand in a format built to be read and signed.

**Product / UI (Elyoun's own SaaS & client systems)** — Dark-first UI kit on the same tokens; gold strictly for primary actions and active states; slate/neutral for everything functional. Calm, precise, never cute under pressure (per the voice guide's UI copy rules).
