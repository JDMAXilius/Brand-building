## 01 — WHAT IS COMPANION GROUP?
> *"Technical minds [POWERED_BY] Human collaboration."* _(verified hero copy)_

Companion Group is an employee-owned game co-development partner that designs, develops, and integrates
the **online services a game depends on** — from off-the-shelf GBaaS (game-backend-as-a-service) to fully
bespoke backends. Its founding bet: the hardest, least-glamorous part of a modern game — multiplayer,
backend, live-service, cloud — is best handled by senior engineers who **embed directly into the client's
team** and either add velocity or own a feature end to end. It positions on *people + craft* ("technical
minds, human collaboration"), not scale or capacity.

## 02 — VISION _(inferred)_
*To be the senior engineering partner AAA and live-service studios trust with the online systems that
make or break a launch.*

## 03 — MISSION _(verified framing)_
*Design, develop, and integrate the online services a game depends on — embedding with the client's team
to augment velocity or take full ownership of end-to-end delivery.*

## 04 — VALUES _(inferred, consistent with verified copy)_
- **Seniority** — principal-level engineers, not headcount.
- **Ownership** — employee-owned; "take full ownership of end-to-end delivery."
- **Human collaboration** — partnership and people are the headline, not just tech.
- **Craft** — the unseen backend done right (netcode, fleets, live-service).

## 05 — GOALS _(inferred)_
- Be the go-to senior backend/multiplayer/online-services partner for AAA & live-service.
- Win and publish case studies that prove the hard systems shipped.
- Grow the senior bench (active hiring) without becoming a volume body shop.

| Dimension | Profile |
|---|---|
| **Buyer titles** | CTO, Technical Director, Lead/Principal Engineer, Studio Head, Exec Producer |
| **Org types** | AAA & live-service game studios and publishers |
| **Geography** | UK/EU footprint (Recruitee careers defaulted to Germany; UK Games Fund linkage) |
| **Problem** | Hard online/multiplayer/backend/cloud work they can't staff or solve in-house |

**Persona — "Technical Director, live-service title"** _(inferred):* "I need senior engineers who can own
multiplayer and the backend — a partner who behaves like a stakeholder, not a vendor of hours." Wants
netcode/backend depth and end-to-end ownership; fears capacity shops that supply hours but not outcomes.

## 07 — PERSONALITY _(verified-refined)_
**Axis:** Specialist · Deeply-technical · Energetic · Partnership-led.
Engineer-to-engineer credibility wrapped in a *friendly, gamer-dev* brand — a mascot ("companion" robot),
"human collaboration," code/mono flourishes (`[00]`, `[+]`, `//`). Warmer and more brand-forward than an
austere, anti-marketing studio.

## 08 — VOICE _(verified-refined)_
*Talks like a principal engineer who loves the craft — specific and credible, but warm ("your companion")
and confident, not dry.*
- **Do:** name the systems (GBaaS, multiplayer, gameplay, cloud); lead with the partnership/ownership
  promise; keep the human + playful note.
- **Don't:** drift into pure jargon or cold understatement — the "human collaboration" warmth is the
  differentiator.
- **Qualities:** Technical · Precise · Owner-minded · Human/playful.

and integrates the online services a game depends on — multiplayer, gameplay, backend, and cloud — embedding

## What Elyoun / Brand B learns from this reconstruction
1. **Companion owns the online/backend systems story deeply** (GBaaS → bespoke backends; Online Services ·
   Multiplayer · Gameplay · Cloud) — but **only** that layer. No immersive, attraction, simulation, or
   cinematics. That boundary is Brand B's wedge: *systems depth + experience craft in one studio.*
2. **Their "augment or take full ownership" is the game-segment twin of Holon's "own the outcome."** Holon
   must differentiate on *breadth* (immersive + multiplayer + cinematics) and the "built like systems /
   ships, scales, stays alive" reliability story, not on the embed-and-own promise alone.
3. **Credibility playbook (verified, shared with Enludio):** both rivals show **case studies**, use **contact
   forms**, and surface **people** (Companion = named contacts + active Recruitee careers; Enludio = team
   page, but an *empty* board). Holon's current mailto-only / no-team / placeholder-work is leaner than both —
   plan to add real case studies, named contacts, and an active careers board as proof is earned.
4. **Visual contrast is a positioning asset.** Companion = **light cream + electric-blue + playful gamer-dev**;
   Enludio = **dark blue + purple/magenta + human-experience**. Holon's **cinematic dark Void + cyan-led
   spectral, engineering-restraint** reads distinct from both — own the "serious, cinematic, built-like-systems"
   lane between Companion's playful-technical and Enludio's experiential.
