## How to read a "brand strategy" entry

Each company is defined on five axes — the same lens Elyoun applies to itself:

- **Positioning** — the space they claim in the buyer's mind.
- **Promise** — the core value/outcome they sell.
- **Differentiator** — why them over the alternative.
- **Voice & tone** — how the brand sounds.
- **Proof** — the credibility engine (clients, contracts, pedigree, scale).

## OVERALL TOP 5 — Recreation Playbook (Context + Prompt Engineering)

> The five most strategically relevant to Elyoun (your 4 references + the premium-consultancy
> benchmark). For each: a **one-to-one brand-strategy reconstruction** plus *how to reproduce
> it* using Elyoun's own skill stack (`brand-strategist`, `brand-voice`, `marketing-context`)
> driven by **context engineering** (the facts you load first) and **prompt engineering**
> (the exact instructions you run).

**The reproducible method (applies to all five):**

1. **Context engineering — build a `*-context.md` first.** Mirror the structure of
   `.agents/brand-context.md` (Brand · Audience · Positioning · Personality · Voice ·
   Values/Mission · Goals). Fill every field from research; mark unknowns `_(inferred)_`.
   The skill reads this file first, so *quality of context = quality of output.*
2. **Prompt engineering — run the skills in order**, each prompt naming the context file,
   the target axis, and the exact format. Constrain voice with explicit do/don't lists.
3. **Verify** against the company's real copy; correct the context; re-run. Iterate.

Generic recreation prompt skeleton (reuse for any competitor):
```
/brand-strategist
Context: load ./<company>-context.md as the single source of truth.
Task: reconstruct <COMPANY>'s brand strategy as a senior strategist report with sections:
positioning statement, promise, 5 brand values, personality axis, voice qualities (with
do/don't), 3 buyer personas, competitor framing, and 5 tagline candidates.
Constraints: B2B, premium, evidence-based. Quote only confirmed copy; mark inferences.
Output: markdown, mirroring BRAND_STRATEGY.md's section style.
```

**Conclusions:**
1. **The "empty center" holds.** No single competitor credibly spans premium AI/software
   consulting **+** owned products **+** immersive/XR **+** defense-grade security. That gap
   is Elyoun's position and it is still open.
2. **The Orlando immersive consortium is the closest real threat** — it already bridges
   theme-park ↔ defense-sim. Elyoun's wedge against it: AI/software-engineering rigor,
   security-by-default, and the consult-and-own-products model none of them have.
3. **Don't fight on others' strongest axis.** Not Side's scale, not the venture studios'
   speed. Compete on *fusion + production-grade + ownership* — the throughline of every
   segment above.
